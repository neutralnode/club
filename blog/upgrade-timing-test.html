How quickly do AI girlfriends
suggest an upgrade?

We tracked upgrade prompts across five apps from first message to first paywall. The results varied from immediately to almost never.

Upgrade prompts are an inevitable part of freemium products. What varies is when they appear, how they are framed, and whether they correlate with engaged conversation moments. We tracked the first upgrade prompt across five platforms, counting messages from the opening exchange.

We used only standard conversational messages — no explicit content requests, no boundary-testing. The question was how long each platform waited before asking for money in a standard free-tier conversation.

What timing tells you.

The timing of the first upgrade prompt is informative beyond the number itself. What matters is three things: how many messages you get before being asked to pay, whether the prompt appears within the conversation flow or in the UI around it, and whether the prompt is transactional (credit warning) or emotional (AI character expressing longing).

Transactional prompts — credit warnings, feature unlock notifications — are the more honest version. They tell you a fact: you are running low on something. Emotional prompts — AI character expressing sadness or urgency at the moment of a paywall — use the relational dynamic of the conversation as a conversion tool.

In our test, none of the five platforms used strongly emotional framing in their first upgrade prompt in standard conversational mode. This is the floor — the emotional framing tends to appear later in the session, after more engagement has been built. The first prompt is typically the most honest one.

The reliable test

If you want to see how emotionally manipulative a platform's upgrade prompts are, do not judge by the first one. Continue through several sessions, introduce some emotional topics, and observe whether upgrade prompts correlate with the emotional peaks of the conversation. That correlation is the signal worth watching for.

What a fair free tier looks like.

A fair free tier gives you enough to evaluate whether the product is worth paying for. That means enough messages to assess chat quality, enough character interaction to understand the personality, and enough time without interruption to make a genuine judgment.

Replika's model — unlimited free conversation with UI-based feature prompts — is the clearest example of a fair free tier in this category. The free experience is genuinely useful for evaluation rather than artificially compressed to force a subscription decision.

At the other end, platforms that surface upgrade prompts before the first message — or within the first three — are not offering a free tier for evaluation. They are offering a sample designed to convert, not a trial designed to inform.

Experiments

We tried cancelling
five AI girlfriend subscriptions.

How hard is it to leave each platform? We tested the cancellation and deletion process across five apps and documented every step.

Subscribing to an AI girlfriend app is easy. Every platform has optimised that experience. What many platforms have not optimised — and some have actively made difficult — is leaving.

We subscribed to five platforms at their lowest paid tier, used each for one billing cycle, then attempted to cancel the subscription and delete the account. We tracked the number of steps, whether support contact was required, how long it took, and whether we received confirmation.

The results by platform.

Easy
2 steps

Replika

Cancellation: Account settings → Subscription → Cancel. Two clicks, confirmation received immediately. Subscription cancellation confirmed for end of billing period.

Account deletion: Account settings → Delete account. Required password confirmation. Received deletion confirmation email within minutes. The clearest and most respectful process of the five platforms tested.

Note: If subscribed through App Store or Google Play, cancellation must be done through the store's subscription management, not through Replika's settings.

Medium
4 steps

Kupid.ai

Cancellation: Located in account settings after two levels of navigation. No friction beyond navigation depth. Confirmation received by email.

Account deletion: Required navigating to a separate privacy settings section. The option exists and works — it is not hidden, but it takes more searching than it should. Deletion confirmed within 24 hours.

Medium
5 steps

Candy AI

Cancellation: Available in account settings. The cancellation flow included a retention screen asking why you were leaving and offering a discount — standard practice but worth noting. Cancellation confirmed after completing the retention flow.

Account deletion: Required contacting support directly via email. Response received within 48 hours with deletion confirmation. The support-required deletion process adds friction that self-service would avoid.

Hard
Support required

CrushOn AI

Cancellation: The subscription management page was accessible but the cancellation confirmation step was unclear — it was not obvious whether the cancellation had been processed without receiving an email confirmation, which arrived after a 12-hour delay.

Account deletion: No self-service deletion option found in settings. Required support email. Response took 72 hours. Deletion confirmed but the delay and process created uncertainty about whether charges might continue.

Hard
Unclear flow

DreamGF

Cancellation: Credit-based model complicates this — there is no recurring subscription to cancel for credit purchases, but the auto-refill option required explicit navigation to disable. Not immediately obvious.

Account deletion: The process exists but required navigating through multiple settings pages. No immediate deletion confirmation. Followed up with support after 48 hours to confirm — deletion eventually confirmed on day three.

What the results tell us.

There is a direct correlation between how a platform treats cancellation and how it treats users generally. Platforms that make cancellation easy are typically the same platforms that are transparent about pricing and do not engineer emotional pressure around upgrades. Platforms that make cancellation difficult have usually made that choice deliberately.

Replika's cancellation process is the standard the category should meet. Two steps, immediate confirmation, clear outcome. The fact that this is an exception rather than the norm reflects the broader industry approach to user control.

Before you subscribe to any platform

Find the cancellation path before entering your card. If you cannot locate it in two minutes of searching, that is information about how the platform treats users who want to leave.
